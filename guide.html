Complete Voter Guide
Everything a first-time or experienced voter needs to know — from registration to casting your vote.
🪪Step 1 — Voter Registration
You must be on the electoral roll to vote. Here's how to register:
01
CHECK IF YOU'RE ALREADY REGISTERED
Visit voters.eci.gov.in and search by name, EPIC number, or mobile number. Many citizens are already enrolled. If found, note your Part Number and Serial Number — you'll need these on election day.
02
FILL FORM 6 (NEW REGISTRATION)
Download Form 6 from voters.eci.gov.in or pick it up from your local ERO/AERO office. Fill in personal details including your permanent address in the constituency where you want to vote.
03
GATHER DOCUMENTS
You need: Proof of Age (10th marksheet, birth certificate, or passport), Proof of Address (Aadhaar, utility bill, or bank passbook), and a recent passport-size photograph.
04
SUBMIT — ONLINE OR OFFLINE
Online: Submit at voters.eci.gov.in with scanned documents.
Offline: Submit at your district ERO office or to your Booth Level Officer (BLO). Ask for an acknowledgment receipt.
05
RECEIVE YOUR EPIC CARD
After verification (4–8 weeks), your EPIC card (Electors Photo Identity Card) is delivered to your address. Keep it safe — this is your primary voter ID. You can also download a digital copy from the DigiLocker app.

🗳️Step 2 — Voting Day Process
What happens at the polling booth from start to finish:
01
FIND YOUR POLLING BOOTH
Check voters.eci.gov.in or call 1950. A voter slip with your booth address may be delivered to your home. Booths are usually schools, community centers, or government buildings within 2 km of your home.
02
ARRIVE WITH YOUR ID
Polling is open 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM. Bring your EPIC card or alternate photo ID. You don't need the voter slip — just your name on the electoral roll is enough. Join the correct gender queue (separate queues for men and women).
03
IDENTITY VERIFICATION
Polling officers verify your identity and find your name in the electoral roll. Your details are marked to prevent double voting. If your name is missing from the roll, you cannot vote — contact the BLO immediately.
04
INDELIBLE INK & SLIP
Indelible ink is applied to your left index finger. This cannot be washed off for 2–3 weeks, preventing double voting. You receive a numbered slip to carry into the polling compartment.
05
CAST YOUR VOTE ON THE EVM
Enter the polling compartment alone (secrecy guaranteed). The EVM's Ballot Unit shows candidate names and party symbols. Press the blue button next to your chosen candidate. A beep confirms your vote.
06
VERIFY YOUR VVPAT SLIP
The VVPAT machine prints a paper slip showing your candidate's name and symbol, visible for 7 seconds through a glass window. Verify this matches your choice. The slip then drops into a sealed box. Your vote is complete!
⚖️Dos and Don'ts on Election Day
Know your rights and responsibilities as a voter:
✅ DO
✓Carry a valid photo ID document
✓Check your name on the electoral roll before election day
✓Vote independently without pressure from anyone
✓Maintain silence and order inside/near the booth
✓Report irregularities on the cVIGIL app (call 1950)
✓Verify your VVPAT slip matches your chosen candidate
✓Help elderly or disabled voters if they need assistance
❌ DON'T
✗Take photos inside the polling booth (illegal)
✗Accept money, gifts, or liquor in exchange for your vote
✗Use your mobile phone inside the polling compartment
✗Vote more than once or on someone else's behalf
✗Engage in campaigning within 200 meters of a booth
✗Carry weapons or inflammatory material to the booth
✗Impersonate another voter (criminal offense)
